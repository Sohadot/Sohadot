Why Sohadot Exists
To bring structure, context, and honesty to a market that often lacks all three.

The domain market suffers from two weak extremes: arbitrary pricing without explanation, and automated tools that flatten everything into shallow scores. Sohadot is being built to occupy a more useful middle ground — where naming quality, buyer logic, lexical legitimacy, and public sales context are treated seriously and explained clearly.

Platform Position
More than a marketplace. Closer to a domain intelligence system.

This is not intended to remain a simple inventory website. It is being shaped as an expanding reference layer around domain names: a place where users can evaluate plausibility, study naming terrain, monitor category signals, explore premium assets, and understand why certain names carry strategic value while others do not.

Core Signals
What the platform takes seriously.
Sohadot’s framework is built around a small number of high-value principles. These principles shape the valuation layer, the keyword layer, the drop watchlists, the portfolio, and the explanatory content across the site.
📚
Lexical legitimacy matters
Dictionary words, rare but legitimate lexical forms, personal names, clean brandables, and weak random strings should not all be treated as if they belong to the same class.
dictionary words rare words names
💼
Commercial intent matters
Some terms imply budgets, infrastructure, software utility, care delivery, payments, trust, or enterprise use. Those signals carry more market weight than vague hype language.
AI finance health B2B
🌐
TLD fit matters
The same naming concept behaves differently across .com, .ai, .io, .co, and other extensions. Good analysis has to respect extension-specific demand and buyer expectations.
.com .ai .io .co
📈
Sales context matters
Publicly reported sales validate real demand and price discovery. They are used as directional market context, not as theatrical proof that every adjacent name deserves the same number.
public comps market context
🧠
Structure matters
Shortness alone is not enough. A name must also be believable, memorable, usable, commercially placeable, and structurally coherent for its intended buyer class.
memorability clarity believability
🤝
Direct and human still matters
Inquiries should not disappear into layers of manipulation or scripted friction. Sohadot is built around direct communication, clear terms, and real transaction practicality.
direct inquiry escrow clarity

Trust Framework
What the site is trying to do correctly.
Trust in this market is not created by louder claims. It is created by better framing, more disciplined language, stronger structure, and clear acknowledgment of uncertainty where uncertainty exists.

Structured, not mystical

Domain analysis is framed through naming logic, commercial use-case, TLD fit, and comparable public context instead of vague “AI magic” or unexplained formula outputs.

Directional, not delusional

Public sales, keyword signals, and valuation outputs are presented as directional market signals. They are not presented as promises of resale outcome or buyer certainty.

Independent, not borrowed authority

Sohadot is an independent platform. It is not presented as affiliated with major registrars or marketplaces unless explicitly stated in context.
