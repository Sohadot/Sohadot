Seller-Approved Escrow Closing Protocol

Sohadot does not process direct payments on-site. Qualified asset transactions may close through independent escrow only after written agreement and seller approval.

Core Positioning

  • Sohadot is not a marketplace.
  • Sohadot does not offer public checkout.
  • No asset can be purchased automatically from the website.
  • Escrow closing is available only after written agreement between buyer and seller.
  • The seller initiates or approves the closing path.
  • The buyer funds escrow through an independent escrow provider.
  • The domain is transferred only after escrow funding is secured.
  • Funds are released only after the agreed transfer process is completed.

The Seven-Step Closing Sequence

Seller-approved closing follows the same sequence every time — no step is skipped, and no step happens automatically without the seller's own approval.

1

Strategic brief requested.

2

Buyer and seller discuss asset, scope, price, and terms in writing.

3

Seller confirms that the asset is eligible for closing.

4

Escrow transaction is initiated or approved by the seller.

5

Buyer funds escrow.

6

Seller transfers the domain through the agreed registrar method.

7

Escrow releases funds after the transfer conditions are satisfied.

What this protocol is not

  • It is not a buy-now flow.
  • It is not a checkout.
  • It is not a public payment mechanism.
  • It is not an automated transfer without seller approval.
  • It is not a pricing table.
  • It is not a guarantee that every inquiry will result in a sale.

How to Start a Closing Review

This page explains the protocol; it does not start it. A Strategic Brief request naming "Escrow closing after written agreement" as the inquiry type is how a serious buyer or seller opens a direct conversation about using this process for a specific asset.
