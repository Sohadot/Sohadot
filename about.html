Our Mission
Bring structure, context, and honesty to a market that is often opaque.
The domain market is full of two bad extremes: arbitrary pricing without explanation, and automated tools that flatten everything into shallow scores. Sohadot.com exists to provide a more disciplined middle ground — where lexical legitimacy, commercial intent, TLD quality, naming structure, and public sales context are treated seriously and explained clearly.

How Sohadot Thinks About Domains
Domains are treated as digital assets, naming systems, and market signals at the same time.
Sohadot’s core approach is that strong domains sit at the intersection of language, commerce, trust, category fit, and timing. That is why the site keeps expanding beyond simple listings into structured tools and reference layers.
Language matters: dictionary words, rare but legitimate words, scientific terms, names, clean brandables, and weak strings should not all be treated as if they belong to the same class.
Commercial intent matters: some terms attract serious buyers because they imply budgets, infrastructure, software utility, care delivery, payments, or trust-based commercial use.
TLD fit matters: the same keyword behaves differently across .com, .ai, .io, .co, and other extensions, and that difference changes market framing.
Sales context matters: reported sales validate real demand and price discovery, but adjacent names do not automatically inherit the same value.
Structure matters: shortness alone is not enough. A name must also be usable, believable, memorable, and commercially placeable.
Why This Matters
A better way to read a domain opportunity
This approach helps Sohadot handle very different naming cases more intelligently: exact-match commercial keywords, rare lexical terms, AI compounds, city domains, founder-style brandables, and public-sales-inspired watchlists. The objective is not to sound clever. The objective is to be genuinely useful.

How Sohadot Frames a Domain
Whether a name is being valued, listed, monitored, or discussed, the same basic framework applies.
1
Lexical Check
Is the string commercially meaningful, lexically legitimate, name-based, rare-but-authentic, invented-but-clean, or simply weak?
2
Market Fit
Does the term map to real buyer categories such as software, finance, health, infrastructure, local utility, AI, or brand-led commercial use?
3
Structural Quality
Length, memorability, readability, extension fit, and naming usability are examined before any pricing logic becomes credible.
4
Comparable Context
Public sales are used as directional proof of market attention and pricing behavior, not as lazy one-to-one guarantees.
How a Sale or Inquiry Typically Works
The operating principle is straightforward: fewer layers, clearer terms, safer transfer, and no ambiguity about process.
1
Inquiry

3

4
Transfer
